# Job 5: Editorial Calendar & Backlog

Design the editorial calendar (visibility) and content backlog (execution system). These are two distinct things that work together.

## Trigger

Human asks Arvin to build or revise the editorial calendar, or when content pillars and channel strategy are established and production needs structure.

## Key Principle

"Calendars = visibility. System = execution. You need both." (Buffer)

A 2-person team cannot commit to a fixed publishing schedule. The backlog is a pull system — pull from it when capacity allows. The calendar is for coordination and visibility across divisions, not for commitments.

## Procedure

1. Read the master strategy, content pillars, and channel strategy
2. Read the acquihire brief for value channel priorities
3. Read the research document — Buffer's editorial workflow, Shape Up's appetite model

4. **Design the content backlog:**
   - Where do ideas get captured? (Single location, low friction)
   - How are ideas categorized? (By pillar, by channel, by value channel served)
   - How does an idea move from backlog to "shaped"? (Brief outline, core argument identified, format chosen)
   - How does a shaped idea get selected? (Betting table — Job 9)
   - What's the appetite for each content type? (LinkedIn post: 1 hour. Blog post: 2 days. Video: 1 week. If it takes longer, circuit breaker.)

5. **Design the calendar:**
   - What cadence per channel/division? (Be realistic for 2 people)
   - How do the three divisions coordinate? (Cross-division visibility, not cross-division commitments)
   - What's the sprint rhythm? (Weekly review — Job 8)
   - What review points exist? (Weekly review, monthly retrospective)

6. **Design the capture-to-publish workflow:**
   - Capture (daily, 5 min) → Transform/Plan (biweekly, 30 min) → Create (weekly, half-day batch) → Prep next week (Friday, 15 min)
   - Adapt Buffer's four-step workflow for LifeBuild's specific situation
