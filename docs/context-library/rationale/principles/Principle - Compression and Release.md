# Principle - Compression and Release

## WHAT: The Principle

The portal pattern -- three ingredients for any threshold moment. Compression: the space before the portal feels contained, intimate, focused. Crossing: a distinct moment of transition. Release: the new space opens up and is genuinely surprising. The compression loads the spring; the contrast creates the feeling. Neither works as well alone.

## WHY: Belief

- Strategy: [[Strategy - Spatial Visibility]] -- spatial impact requires emotional contrast; the map reveal means nothing without compression first
- Driver: Derived from Frank Lloyd Wright's architectural compression-release pattern. Without compression, the map reveal is just a screen loading. WITH compression -- the intimate moment with Jarvis, the single question, the brief enclosed space -- the map reveal becomes a gasp. The gasp is what opens the attention that makes everything after it stick. Examples: Myst (library to island), Breath of the Wild (cave to Hyrule), a forest path narrowing before opening onto a hidden lake.

## HOW: Application

### The Three Ingredients

1. **Compression** -- enclosed, intimate, focused. Attention narrows. The builder cannot see what's coming.
2. **Crossing** -- brief but distinct. The builder knows they're moving between worlds.
3. **Release** -- surprising expansion. The builder couldn't have predicted what's on the other side.

Target feeling when release lands: "Oh -- there's a world here." The gasp does WORK -- it establishes the entire mental model. Feeling and understanding happen simultaneously.

Critical rule: The compression must genuinely obscure what's coming. If the builder can see the whole world from the start, there's no release. Glimpses are fine (they build anticipation), but the full picture must be earned by crossing the threshold.

### What Following This Looks Like

- Builder opens the app, sees an intimate space with Jarvis, answers one question, the portal opens, the map reveals. The gasp.
- The campfire hides the map. The builder's view is narrowed. When the view expands, the contrast creates impact.
- A narrow forest path opens suddenly onto a hidden lake. Compression (the path) makes the release (the lake) feel vast.

### What Violating This Looks Like

- Showing the full dashboard on first load (no compression). The builder sees everything immediately and feels nothing.
- A loading screen instead of a portal (no crossing). Technical wait is not emotional transition.
- The builder seeing the complete map from the start (no surprise in the release). Without hidden-then-revealed, there is no contrast.
- Skipping the campfire and dropping builders directly on the map. Without compression, the map is just a screen, not a world.

### Tensions

- With immediacy -- compression takes time (roughly 60 seconds). Resolution: the compression IS the builder's first action (typing an answer), so the time is active, not idle.
- With transparency -- hiding the map to create surprise could feel manipulative. Resolution: the compression is genuine (an intimate moment with Jarvis), not artificial delay.

### Test

Does the builder gasp? (Metaphorically or literally.) If the reveal produces no emotional contrast, the compression was insufficient or the release was predictable.
